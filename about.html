About this website

This site serves as a place where I learn while writing and you learn by reading afterwards. Every information written down is gathered either from my own experience, with a help of the web or other resources. I see this as a great opportunity to learn, while teaching other people who are as interested in the topic as I am. Which by the way I think is awesome.

Philosophy

Of cource, when developing some more serious project, there has to be a certain "goal". This website is not an exception. The goal is simply to not give up in the process! What do I mean by that? Well, to not abandon this project!

I see this as a great responsibility when writing down something, which others may derive from. That's why I really pay attention on what kind of information I'm putting down. With that said, I'd like to apologize for any mistakes, I'd be shamed! ;)

About the author

My personal story

I've always been interested in the technical/internal stuff, how things work under the hood. As a 13-yo, I played CS 1.6 alot. Older games such as this one were very open in a way of modification and configuration. I remember creating my own configs that were HUGE, trying to learn every command in the game. After some time when I learned how to completly modify the game without digging into the internal stuff, I simply wanted more. That's when the cheating era for me started.

When I was 15, I downloaded a source code for public cheat called Vermillion, started to modify it and see the result. By doing this I was slowly making progress towards learning C++. However the progress was slow and time consuming. If I had to do it again, I'd have done it differently. But after two years I learned most of the language. At that time, I've also read a 1,000-page book about C++ which helped me alot. Now I knew 90% of the language, and also many ways how to develop cool stuff into the game. After this, I wanted to learn more.

Each year, I'd make a new cheat. Since these projects were all learning projects, they weren't quite successful, and I wasn't really happy about them. The codebase was bad-organized, I used to change code style alot throuout the years, the quality was bad due to my lack of knowledge at that time, etc. So that's why I started making new cheat every year, which was better and better. (Now btw making the 4th cheat in the 4th year)

When I was around 17-18, I started learning reverse-engineering. After a year or so, I was able to reverse-engineer most of the stuff, so I ran into a bigger project and that was, reverse-engineer the entire GoldSource engine for CS 1.6. And so my childish "dreams" came true, because with such opportunity having actual game code in my hands, I could start modify the game as I wish. Many many stressful weeks and months of reverse-engineering me and my partner, who was helping me, got pretty much close to the original version of the game. We were slowly making our way towards the finish. With a help of public sources of some modules we put together all the parts. However, the engine, launcher, gameUI stuff and more had to be still reversed. And that was what we were focusing on. Eventually, with our reversed client we could connect to an existing server and actually play on it with all the players online.

That was huge, and I think nobody had ever done such thing before. The project is done by 90% but I've left it. We have done most of the hard work, and now it's just waiting for somebody with passion to take it and finish it.

And there I am right now! At the time, I'm focusing on other stuff. For example learning full x86 assembly, making my own really high-quality cheat, which is my personal favourite, writing other smaller projects, building up my github profile, and of cource, writing this website. Hope you enjoy!
